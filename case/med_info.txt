1. LIDOCAINE 2% INJ.
Description: An amide-type local anesthetic used for infiltration and nerve block anesthesia in dental procedures.
Onset of Action: Rapid, typically within 2-5 minutes.
Duration: Provides pulpal anesthesia for approximately 60 minutes.
Indications: Routine dental extractions, cavity preparations, and minor oral surgery.
Contraindications: Known hypersensitivity to amide-type anesthetics, severe heart block (atrioventricular block), or acute heart failure. Use with caution in patients with liver disease as amide anesthetics are metabolized in the liver.

2. SEPTANEST WITH ADRENALINE (Articaine 4% + Epinephrine 1:100,000)
Description: A potent local anesthetic combining Articaine (4%) with Epinephrine (1:100,000 or 1:200,000) as a vasoconstrictor.
Clinical Advantage: High lipid solubility allows better penetration into bone tissue, making it ideal for complex extractions and long procedures.
Precautions: Use with extreme caution in patients with hypertension, cardiac arrhythmias, or coronary artery disease due to the epinephrine content.
Contraindications: Strictly prohibited for patients with untreated thyrotoxicosis (overactive thyroid) or those with known sulfite allergies (contains sodium metabisulfite as a stabilizer). Avoid use in areas with limited collateral circulation.

3. ETHANOL 96% DENATURED
Description: High-concentration denatured alcohol used strictly for surface disinfection and cleaning of non-critical dental instruments.
Safety Warning: Highly flammable liquid. Store in a cool, well-ventilated area away from open flames or electrical sparks.
Usage: Not for internal use or application on mucous membranes. Contact with oral mucosa can cause severe chemical burns.

4. COMPOSITE FILLING (A2 SHADE)
Description: A light-cured, resin-based dental restorative material used for aesthetic fillings in both anterior and posterior teeth.
Storage: Must be stored in a dark place at room temperature (below 25°C).
Technical Note: Highly sensitive to ambient light. Exposure to operatory lights or sunlight will trigger premature polymerization (hardening), rendering the material unusable. Ensure the cap is secured immediately after use.
Contraindications: Do not use over zinc oxide eugenol bases — eugenol inhibits the polymerization of resin composites.

5. ISOPROPYL ALCOHOL 70%
Description: A mid-concentration alcohol used for surface disinfection of environmental surfaces and non-critical dental instruments.
Safety Warning: Flammable liquid. Keep away from heat sources, open flames, and electrical equipment. Store in a sealed container in a cool, ventilated area.
Usage Limitations: Not recommended as a skin antiseptic before injections due to insufficient residual antimicrobial activity. Avoid contact with acrylic or composite resin surfaces — prolonged exposure causes surface degradation and discolouration.
Contraindications: Do not apply to open wounds or mucous membranes. Not suitable for sterilization — it is a disinfectant only.

6. UBISTESIN 4% WITH ADRENALINE (Articaine 4% + Epinephrine 1:200,000)
Description: A local anesthetic combining Articaine (4%) with a lower concentration of Epinephrine (1:200,000) compared to Septanest. Suitable for longer procedures where a reduced vasoconstrictor dose is preferred.
Clinical Advantage: Lower epinephrine concentration reduces cardiovascular risk, making it preferable for patients with mild cardiovascular disease where a vasoconstrictor is still clinically necessary.
Precautions: Same class as Septanest — use with caution in patients with hypertension, cardiac arrhythmias, or thyroid conditions.
Contraindications: Contraindicated in untreated thyrotoxicosis and in patients with known sulfite allergies (contains sodium metabisulfite). Avoid in areas with severely compromised circulation.

7. SODIUM HYPOCHLORITE 5.25%
Description: The primary irrigant for root canal treatment (endodontics). Effectively dissolves organic tissue (pulp remnants, necrotic debris) and provides broad-spectrum antibacterial action.
Usage: Used exclusively for intracanal irrigation during root canal procedures. Must be delivered carefully to avoid extrusion beyond the apex.
Safety Warning: Cytotoxic to periapical tissues. Accidental injection into periapical tissue or soft tissue causes severe pain, swelling, and tissue necrosis. Always use with a side-vented needle and passive delivery pressure.
Contraindications: Hypersensitivity to hypochlorites. Never used for surface disinfection of instruments or patient skin — it is corrosive and damages many materials.
Storage: Store in a dark, cool environment. Degrades in light and heat, losing antimicrobial efficacy over time.

8. CALCIUM HYDROXIDE PASTE (Ca(OH)2)
Description: An intracanal medicament placed between endodontic appointments to maintain an alkaline environment, inhibit bacterial regrowth, and promote periapical healing.
Additional Use: Used for direct and indirect pulp capping procedures in vital pulp therapy.
Mechanism: High pH (approximately 12.5) denatures bacterial enzymes and promotes hard tissue formation.
Safety Note: Generally considered biocompatible. However, prolonged contact (several months or more) with dentinal walls may cause cervical root fracture due to dentine dehydration. Remove completely before obturation.
Contraindications: None significant at clinical concentrations. Avoid contact with eyes.

9. CHLORHEXIDINE GLUCONATE 0.12%
Description: An antiseptic oral rinse used pre- and post-operatively to reduce oral bacterial load and support wound healing after extractions or oral surgery.
Mechanism: Broad-spectrum antiseptic active against gram-positive bacteria, gram-negative bacteria, and some fungi. Substantivity (residual activity on oral surfaces) lasts up to 12 hours.
Side Effects: Temporary brown staining of teeth and tongue with prolonged use. Altered or bitter taste perception during treatment. Rarely, allergic reactions (urticaria, contact dermatitis).
Contraindications: Known hypersensitivity to chlorhexidine gluconate. Avoid contact with eyes and ears. Not for injection or ingestion.
Usage: Instruct patient to rinse for 30 seconds twice daily. Do not eat or drink for 30 minutes after use.

10. GLASS IONOMER CEMENT — TYPE II (RESTORATIVE)
Description: A fluoride-releasing restorative material that bonds chemically to enamel and dentine without the need for an adhesive system. The fluoride release provides ongoing caries protection around the restoration margins.
Indications: Class V cavities (cervical erosion lesions), root caries in elderly patients, and restorations in low stress-bearing areas. Also used as a liner or base under composite resin.
Application Note: Acid etching is NOT required. Condition the cavity with 10% polyacrylic acid for 10-20 seconds only. Prolonged conditioning weakens the bond.
Limitations: Lower fracture toughness and wear resistance than composite resin. Not recommended for load-bearing posterior restorations (occlusal surfaces of molars) or areas of heavy occlusal contact.
Storage: Store components (powder and liquid) separately at room temperature. Do not refrigerate the liquid.

11. HYDROGEN PEROXIDE 3%
Description: A mild antiseptic solution used for wound irrigation in oral surgery and as an auxiliary irrigant during root canal procedures (in combination with sodium hypochlorite).
Usage: Apply with a syringe or soaked gauze for short-contact wound irrigation. Effervescence (oxygen release) mechanically dislodges debris from wound sites.
Safety: At 3% concentration, considered low-risk. Avoid prolonged contact with oral mucosa — it can cause superficial mucosal irritation and temporary whitening.
Contraindications: Do not use in deep, closed, or undermined wounds — released oxygen can cause subcutaneous emphysema or air embolism. Do not use concentrations above 3% without specific clinical indication and additional safety protocols.
Storage: Keep in a tightly sealed, opaque container away from light and heat. Degrades over time — discard if no effervescence is observed upon testing.

12. ZINC OXIDE EUGENOL CEMENT (TEMPORARY RESTORATION)
Description: A two-component temporary restorative cement (zinc oxide powder + eugenol liquid) used for temporary cavity sealing and as a pulp sedative dressing in symptomatic teeth.
Clinical Benefit: The eugenol component provides analgesic and mild anti-inflammatory effect on the exposed pulp, reducing patient sensitivity between appointments.
Setting Time: Approximately 3-5 minutes in the oral environment (humidity accelerates setting).
Critical Contraindication: Incompatible with resin composite restorations. Residual eugenol on cavity walls inhibits the polymerization of composite resin, leading to soft, under-cured restorations. Always remove all traces and apply a eugenol-free base before placing composite.
Additional Contraindication: Some patients exhibit contact sensitivity to eugenol. Discontinue use if allergic reaction occurs.
